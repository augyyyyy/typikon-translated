## A

### Ancient Liturgical Gospels (Ostroh, Vatican)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Readings & Scriptures*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 93, L102*

[93] The most important readings, without doubt, -- are the Sunday and Saturday ones, since in the most ancient Gospels readings are given only for these two days of the week, so in the Ostroh one and in two Vatican ones: one Greek of the 9th century and one Slavonic Glagolitic of the 11th century.

## B

### Biblical Odes (Odes of Holy Scripture)
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Part 1 Structure, L174*

Although now we do not use the Odes of Holy Scripture presented at the end of the Psalter, excluding the Forty Days, nevertheless, we leave something from them, that is at the 8th Ode, instead of Glory - the refrain 'Let us bless the Father, and the Son, and the Holy Spirit, the Lord,' and the refrain to the Katavasia of the 8th Ode: 'We praise, we bless, we worship the Lord, praising and magnifying Him unto all ages,' which is sung according to the tone of the Katavasia; also the Song of the Theotokos 'My soul magnifies' before the irmos of the 9th Ode of the canon, which we take also in the time of the Forty Days, and outside of it, with the exception of Feasts of the Lord and of the Theotokos, on which, according to ancient typikons, it is not proper to take [it].

---

### Biblical Odes in the Triodion Canon
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Part 4 Triodion, L211*

6. Odes and Canons. The Biblical Odes, given at the end of the Psalter, are intertwined with the odes of the canon, as given: before the ode of the canon the corresponding ode of the Psalter is recited, leaving its last verses for refrains to the troparia of the canon, in such quantity as there will be troparia in the ode of the canon, including the heirmos, except two, for to the penultimate troparion we refrain "Glory", and to the last - "Both now". The 2nd Ode is recited in full before the ode of the canon, and to the troparia of its canon we refrain: "Glory to Thee, our God, glory to Thee". According to our ancient custom, the Odes of the Psalter are recited only in those odes in which the triodion occurs, and in the other odes we refrain the usual refrains to the troparia of the canon. The Greek Typikon prescribes singing the Odes of the Psalter in the tone of the Menaion canon, but now we do not even know what their melody was.

### Blessing of Water on August 1
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 454, L475*

[454] Although Greek typikons do not give the blessing of water on this day, however, this custom, inherent to Greek-Slavs, is taken from the Greeks, who once had the custom of performing the blessing of water at the beginning of each month and, accordingly, on 1 August, according to the testimony of Balsamon in the explanation of Canon V of the Trullan Synod: Haec statuente canone, novilunium quidem festum ante annos omnem memoriam supeantes cessavit; eius loco, Dei gratia, ut et ipse propitious reddatur, preces, as Aquae lustrales in Ecclesia a populo fideli fiunt in uniuscuiusque mensis principio (Asseman. Calend. Tom. I. Cap. XI. Pag. 156) /Establishing this canon, truly the new moon feast, which from ancient times erased all memory of it; in its place, thanks be to God, and better prayers take place, and sanctification of water in the church, which the faithful people perform at the beginning of each month (Assemani, Calend., Vol. III, Part I, Chapter XI, p. 156)/.

## C

### Canon for the Dead and Panikhida
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 107, L117*

[107] Slavonic typikons, in order not to discard the canon for the dead, transfer it to the Panikhida over the Kolyva, or to Compline (Pochaiv Octoechos at the end and Moscow Typikon, chapter 12). The Greek Typikon in chapter 6, instead of the Martyria canon, prescribes the canon for the dead; however, with such a decision the Martyria canon would never be taken: neither on these Saturdays, nor on Souls Saturdays, for even on these Saturdays that same Typikon does not prescribe the Martyria canon. Probably ordinary Octoechoi did not have it.

---

### Canon Structure (Litanies, Hymns, Sessional Hymns, Kontakia, Exaposteilaria)
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Part 1 Structure, L176*

After the 3rd, 6th and 9th Ode of the canon, immediately after the Katavasia, the Small Litany is taken (according to the model given at the first Kathisma of Matins), each with its own exclamation, and after the litany at the 3rd Ode the Sessional Hymn is sung, at the 6th - Kontakion and Ikos, and at the 9th - the Exaposteilarion (Hymn of Light) is read. Both the Kontakion and Ikos are taken once each, the Sessional Hymn on Feasts of the Lord and of the Theotokos - twice, that is once - simply the Sessional Hymn, and the second time - with the refrain Glory, Both now. And the Exaposteilarion on Feasts of the Lord and of the Theotokos is taken three times, that is twice - simply, and the third time - with the refrain Glory, Both now. On the Feast of a Polyeleos or non-Polyeleos Saint, which is celebrated, according to the Slavonic Typikon, on 6, the Sessional Hymn and his Exaposteilarion are taken twice , then Both now: his Theotokion. If the Saint has two Sessional Hymns or Exaposteilaria, then they are taken once each, that is the first - simply, the second - with the refrain Glory, then Both now: his Theotokion . When several services coincide, each with its own Sessional Hymn, Kontakion-Ikos and Exaposteilarion, then they are taken one after another, according to the order of services; however, the Kontakion-Ikos of the first service is taken at the 6th Ode in its place, and the Kontakion-Ikos of the second, according to the Slavonic typikons , is transferred to the 3rd Ode and goes immediately before the Sessional Hymn. The sequence of Sessional Hymns and Exaposteilaria in the middle of a Feast of the Lord and of the Theotokos ends with the Sessional Hymn or Exaposteilarion of the Feast. After the Katavasia of the 9th Ode, according to the Slavonic Typikon, 'It is truly meet' is taken, if it is not Sunday. If it is Sunday, 'It is truly meet' is not taken, but then, between the Small Litany and the Exaposteilarion (Exapostolarion) we sing, according to the tone of the Prokeimenon of the Liturgy, the troparion 'Holy is the Lord our God' (3)" .

---

### Censing at the Litiya (Lytia)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 14, L15*

^[14] Since no Typikon (the Synod testifies) prescribes censing in the temple here, it would be better if the deacons cense from the place of the Litiya when they arrive there. And since this is better, we have placed such a rubric in the text, and we have completely rejected the worse existing local custom of censing the Holy Table and the local icons as at "Lord, I have cried," although the Synod mentions it too. However, if there are icons in the narthex, it is proper to cense them first, for the ancient Typikons prescribe so.

---

### Compline and Midnight Office on Feasts without Vigil
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 341, L362*

[341] Since ancient typikons do not give All-Night Vigil, nothing is mentioned about the troparia at the blessing of loaves; but to sing: troparion to the saint once, Glory, Both now: to the feast, as the Pochaiv Anthologion has, does not correspond to the rite, according to which troparia are taken at the All-Night Vigil. -- "If there is no All-Night Vigil, at Compline -- Kontakion to the Saint, Glory, Both now: of the Feast". At Midnight Office, after the 1st Trisagion -- Troparion of the Feast; after the 2nd -- Kontakion of the Feast; we do not say the prayer "Remember", but "Lord, have mercy" (12) and dismissal. Thus in Slavic menaia.

## D

### Daily Vespers (without Kathisma)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Part 1 Structure, L92*

This Vespers is celebrated just as Daily [Vespers] , however, without a Kathisma and without litanies, except for the last 'Have mercy on us, O God,' which only the Slavonic Typikons present, but the Greek do not prescribe. The stichera at 'Lord, I have cried' will be only 4. The dismissal, although it can begin from 'Glory to Thee, O Christ God,' yet with us is exclaimed also before that: 'Wisdom!' and 'More honorable' just as at Daily Vespers.

---

### Dismissals (Feast vs. Day Commemorations)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 317, L337*

[317] The ancient Lviv Horologion gives the dismissal of the feast, however, since in all other cases dismissals of feasts begin with Vespers of the feast, rightly the Tserkovne Oko and Pochaiv Menaion give "dismissal of the day", with commemoration, as it seems to us, also of the saint who falls on this day.

### Dogmatikon and Afterfeasts on Fridays
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 376, L397*

[376] Thus in chapter 43 of Mark, Tserkovne Oko, Pochaiv Menaion and Moscow Typikon; however, if there is to be uniformity in typikons, then we think that here too the 1st Theotokion of the tone should be according to the general rule of the Afterfeast of the Lord and the Theotokos, given at the end of the Pochaiv and Moscow Octoechos under the prescription about the temple of the Resurrection of Christ; and if -- not, then also in the Afterfeast of other feasts there should be a stichera of the Feast, which would be in greater conformity with ancient typikons, in which neither on the Forefeast, nor on the Afterfeast, nor on the Apodosis of the Feast is the Dogmatikon given on Friday evening, but only stichera of the Feast, regardless -- whether it is Saturday or another day of the week. (Our handwritten Typikon, chapter 20 and chapter 11 of Mark in the Tserkovne Oko), although general Slavic typikons on the Fore- and Afterfeast and on the Apodosis of the Feast give the Dogmatikon. Moscow Typikon 12 September, 24 March, 14 August; Tserkovne Oko 12 September, 21 September; Pochaiv Menaion 12 September; Typikon of the Lviv University, p. 87.

### Exaposteilaria, Kontakia, and Theotokia after Canons
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 127, L139*

[127] Moscow Typikon, chapter 4, and Peremyshl, page 85. -- Mark in chapters 78 and 79 prescribes after the 3rd Ode the Sessional Hymn of the saint, Glory, Both now: 1st Theotokion of the tone, that is Sunday, after the 6th -- Kontakion of the saint. New Constantinopolitan Typikon after the 3rd Ode: Κοντακιον το αναστασιμον χύμα, είτα ψαλλεται το Κάθισμα του Αγιου, και το θεοτοκιον; μετά δε την εκτην ωδην Κοντακιον και Οίκος του εορταζομενου Αγιου /Kontakion of the resurrection spoken, then is sung the Kathisma of the saint, and the Theotokion; after the 6th Ode Kontakion and Ikos of the celebrated Saint/. ## F

### Feast of St. Catherine (Transfer of Feasts)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Monastic & Parish Celebrations*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 279, L298*

[279] We find the Service of St. Catherine as having Polyeleos only in our Pochaiv Menaion and in the new Greek-Venetian one; but in others -- without Polyeleos. In later Greek typikons we find it given on 25 November with the Apodosis of the Feast of the Entry into the Temple of the Most Holy Theotokos, and in earlier ones -- on the 24th, as it is today among Greek-Slavs. Thus the Greek-Venetian Menaion of 1615 gives: Σημειωσαι ουν, ότι ευρομεν εν τισι Τυπικοις των νεωτέρων, ότι εν τιοα των πόλεων ψαλλεται η ακολουθία της αγίας μάρτυρος Αικατερίνης εν τη κε του μηνός, εν η και αποδιδοται η εορτή. Εν ετεροις δε Τυπικοις παλαιοτεροις ευρομεν αυτήν εορτάζεσαι εν τη κδ του αυτού μηνός: εν δε τη κε ψαλλομεν την ακολουθίαν του εν αγιοις Κλημεντος Παπα Ρώμης, και Πέτρου Αλεξανδρείας, εν η και αποδιδοται η εορτή των Εισοδιων /Note, therefore, that we found in some newer typikons, that in some places the service of the holy martyr Catherine is sung on the 25th day of this month, on which the feast is also given up. In other older typikons we found that her memory is celebrated on the 24th of this month, and on the 25th we sing the service of Saints Clement Pope of Rome and Peter of Alexandria, and on this day also the feast of the Entry is given up/. This transfer from the 24th to the 25th day, in the opinion of the Greeks, as explained by the new Greek-Venetian Menaion (24 November, p. 158), was done at the wish of the monks of Mount Sinai, so that it would not coincide with the Apodosis of the feast and, so that in this way the memory of the saint would be honored more solemnly. For we know that the body of the holy great martyr was transferred by angelic power to Mount Sinai and buried there.

## H

### Holy Doors vs. Royal Doors (Historical Usage & Rubrics)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Objects & Architecture*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 264, L283*

[264] Ancient Greek typikons mention nothing about a deacon, neither at the bringing out, nor at the exaltation, nor at the laying of the precious Cross, but only about one priest. The Greek typikon, similarly to the Slavic ones, prescribes for the priest to vest in all priestly vestments: αλασσει ο Ιερεύς πασαν την ιερατικην στολην /the priest vests in all priestly vestments/. The Greek Triodion (Sunday of the Veneration of the Cross) and the new Constantinopolitan Typikon, similarly to Slavic typikons, prescribes for the priest to enter with the cross from the sanctuary to the temple through the northern doors: εκ του αριστερού Βήματος /from the left side of the sanctuary/. Greek typikons, with the exception of the new Constantinopolitan, and the Slavic of the Lviv University, prescribe for the priest, having exited from the sanctuary to the temple, to head to the entrance doors that lead from the temple to the narthex, which are sometimes called royal, and there await the end of the Doxology and from there exclaim "Wisdom, attend", and then head to the tetrapod, which is before the iconostasis. Although these doors are called royal, βασιλικαι /royal/, one must understand that they were called so in the past. Today we call royal the middle doors of the iconostasis, which according to ancient custom are called holy: αγιαι ΰυραι /holy doors/ (chapter 96, Greek Typikon).

---

### Holy Doors vs. Royal Doors (Terminology)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Objects & Architecture*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote [754], Appendix, L66*

[754] Ed. note. In ancient times, "Royal Doors" referred to those doors that led from the narthex to the temple, for at them the Patriarch met the Emperor. Later this name was applied with improper meaning to the middle doors of the iconostasis. In the rubrics of modern Greek liturgical books, "holy doors" are given (see Akolouthia tou Esperinou kai tou Orthrou, Roma 1950, p. 16), therefore we also consistently correct "Royal Doors" to "Holy Doors" everywhere in this Typikon.

### Holy Liturgy (Concelebration & Repetition)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Monastic & Parish Celebrations*
- **Source Reference:** *Point 97, Appendix, L275*

97. According to the ancient custom of the Eastern Church, only one Holy Liturgy may be celebrated at the same holy table. ### Kathisma 'Blessed is the Man' on Feasts
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 277, L296*

[277] Some consider, according to the edition of the Peremyshl Typikon of 1870, p. 271, that neither on St. Michael, nor on St. Prophet Elias, should "Blessed is the man" be sung and, that then not a single kathisma is taken, for this kathisma, as the publisher writes about it, is found only in the Pochaiv Anthologion, and in the most ancient menaia and Anthologia they are not mentioned; the reason for this is considered to be that "Elias did not die, and St. Michael, as bodiless, is not subject to death". About St. Elias it is written in the Pochaiv Menaion: "Let it be known that in printed Greek menaia, in handwritten typikons, and also in Slavic handwritten and printed typikons, the service of the Prophet Elias is given on 6, with Octoechos". ### Kathismata and Small Litanies at Matins
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 77, L86*

[77] When two kathismata should be read, and when three, we noted in the note to the Order of Daily Matins on page 41. Conversely, in the Russian Typikon, approved by their Governing Synod (Moscow edition of 1859), in the Order of Daily Matins it is written that the priest says them only at the Vigil and when the memory of saints having a Polyeleos and Doxology is celebrated; similarly also in the new Wallachian-Bucharest Typikon, in which in the Order of Daily Matins is given: "We take sessional hymns from the Octoechos without litanies" (untranslatable); and adds: "In monasteries, usually, between kathismata litanies are taken only on Sunday and when more special saints occur". From the Greek Typikon no conclusion can be made, for in it small litanies are not mentioned neither at Small Matins, nor at Great, nor before the Canon, nor after the Canon. It is assumed in the Typikon that this is known to everyone. From the rubrics of the Greek Triodion, given at Matins on Meatfare Saturday, one can conclude that these litanies should be said also at Small Matins. For it gives: η συνηιϊης στιχολογια. In all small litanies of the Stichology and of the Canon, all our fathers and brethren who have passed away before us are commemorated/. Let everyone observe the rubric and custom of his Church. Finally, in our opinion, later typikons reject litanies here only for the sake of greater shortening of the divine service; if the single difference between Small and Great Matins were the rejection or use of litanies, then this difference should have been indicated in some place of ancient liturgical books; however, this is not encountered. What the exclamation of the third litany should be is not indicated in any of the church books. Therefore we are forced to borrow it from Great Matins. At Great Matins in Slavonic books two exclamations are given which are used at the third litany, that is one -- for Sunday "For blessed is" and the second -- for Polyeleos feasts "For holy Art Thou". For the sake of distinguishing the Sunday day from the ordinary day, at Small Matins it is appropriate to use the second "For holy Art Thou", although it would not be bad to use also the first "For blessed is" (Οτι ευλογητοα), for in Greek books we find only this one exclamation, without distinction of day. Regarding Sessional Hymns, they are taken two or three after each stichology, depending on the edition, except the Theotokion. In Lviv editions of the Octoechos and in the Greek-Venetian one, only to the Theotokion is sung "Glory, Both now". In the Pochaiv and Moscow ones a separate refrain is also given to every second, or even to the third sessional hymn. This refrain, corresponding to the content of the sessional hymn: on Monday and Tuesday -- Penitential "O Lord, rebuke me not in Thine anger"; on Wednesday and Friday -- of the Cross, and on Thursday -- of the Apostles; to the third sessional hymn, that is the Martyron -- also the Martyron refrain: "Wondrous is God in His saints".

### Liturgical Readings (Prokeimena, Alleluias, Apostle-Gospel, Communion Hymns)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Readings & Scriptures*
- **Source Reference:** *Part 2 General Rubrics, L123*

7.	If there be several Prokeimena, Alleluias, Apostle-Gospels and Communion Hymns, then one must know how to take them. Know first, that in church typikons more than two are not given, with the exception of Apostle-Gospels, of which only in Slavonic typikons three are given, for the Slavs are richer in supplications than the Greeks. For where three readings coincide, the Greek typikons do not give the sequential one, but the Slavonic ones take it under the refrain of the first reading, as if one with it . When this is impossible, on the occasion of some great feast, then they order to take it on the day before under the refrain with the sequential one . However, this rule has been preserved among us perhaps only in cathedral churches, for in others usually only one Apostle-Gospel is taken, as also one service either of the day, or of the saint, or one for need. If, therefore, there be two Prokeimena and two Alleluias, then both are taken under one heading, that is both Prokeimena - before the refrain of the first reading, both Alleluias - after the completion of the second reading. However, the first Prokeimenon is taken twice, namely: once - before and once - after the verse, the second - only once and without a verse, for in this case the Typikon does not give a verse. Similarly also "Alleluia": both of its verses are taken only of the first service, and of the second - only one first verse . The Heading, that is from whom: from Paul, from Matthew or from another, is taken only of the first reading, and the others usually are not taken, although it would be better to announce the headings of other readings as well, for it is not indifferent to the listeners to know the reading of which holy author is being proclaimed; and for this reason only would the rubric make certain sense, which orders to take the sequential reading under the refrain, for taking one reading under the refrain of another means merging it with the previous one because its heading is not announced. If the heading of the following third reading, which is not taken under the refrain, is not named, then it should have been said that this third reading is also taken under the refrain. "Let us be attentive" is announced only at the beginning of the first reading. If there be two Communion Hymns, then "Alleluia" is repeated only upon the completion of the second .

3.	In older Menaia, which our Menaia also follow, troparia and kontakia are not given to every Saint, but in closer times we find them given in our Horologia. Therefore one should not wonder if regarding the use of troparia at the Hours the rubrics in some way do not agree with the Menaion and our Horo- or Prayer Book. For when a Saint had a troparion and kontakion, according to the ancient Typikon, they were taken at all Hours; but according to the Typikon of our Horo- and Prayer Books, in order not to repeat at all Hours on all days one and the same troparion and kontakion, for a certain variety at the 1st Hour - the troparion and kontakion of the day of the week, at the 3rd and 9th - of the Saint, and at the 6th - of the Temple.

### Troparia of the Day, Temple, and Saints
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 20, L21*

^[20] However, it is better presented in the Greek Horologia -- only one troparion, that is of the current Saint, or the four aforementioned: "O God of our Fathers" with the three others, without distinction of day. In the Greek Menaia the Saint does not always have a troparion and, when he does not have one, then the general troparia are taken -- "O God of our Fathers"; when he has one, then to the Saint. With us, because the current Saint in the Menaion always has a troparion, then, if it were taken always, the troparia "O God of our Fathers" would be superfluous; so that they not be superfluous, they are taken daily, and to the Saints -- only to the Polyeleos ones. The troparion of the day and of the Temple is given only in the Slavonic Typikons.

### Troparion and Kontakion of Forefeast and Saints
- **Category:** *Hymnographic Elements & Structures*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 180, L196*

[180] According to the previous typikon: troparion of the Forefeast and to the saint, if he has, Glory: Kontakion to the saint, if he has, Both now: Kontakion of the Forefeast; everything else -- only to the saint, and Apostle-Gospel also of the day, in the first place. Also to the second saint is taken, if he has something. Before the troparia of the saint Slavonic typikons prescribe to the temple of the saint (Pochaiv Triodion, fol. 11, on the verso.).

### Typikons
- **Category:** *History & Rite Identification*
- **Source Reference:** *Intro, L215*

Ritual unity is achieved through the observance of unified rubrical prescriptions. From ancient times, the Eastern Churches began to use collections of such prescriptions, that is, Typikons (from the Greek word typikon).

## V

### Vespers and Vigil on the Eves of Nativity and Theophany
- **Category:** *Liturgical Services & Offices*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 318, L338*

[318] According to our most general and Russian typikons, Vespers with the Service of Basil on the Eve of the Nativity of Christ begins at the seventh hour of the day (At one in the afternoon), and on the Eve of Theophany -- at the fifth hour of the day (at eleven before noon, according to the present calculation); but according to other most ancient typikons in both cases: περί ωραν δεκατην της ημέρας/around the tenth hour of the day/ (at four in the afternoon). All-Night Vigil according to the general Slavic [typikon], among which also Tserkovne Oko -- at the tenth hour of the night (that is in the morning); according to others, the most ancient, on the Eve of the Nativity: περί ωραν πρωτην της νυκτός /around the first hour of the night/, and Theophany: περί ωραν δεύτερον της νυκτός /around the second hour of the night/. In the Constantinopolitan Patriarchate Vespers begins immediately after the ninth Royal Hour.

### Weekday Liturgy Commemorations (Greek vs. Slavonic)
- **Category:** *Liturgical Monastic & Parish Celebrations*
- **Source Reference:** *Footnote 91, L100*

[91] The Greek Typikon gives the whole service only to the saint, and Apostle and Gospel -- first of the day, and then of the saint; Προκειμενον του κατά την ημεραν Αγιου, Αλληλούια του Αγιου, Ευαγγελιον της ημέρας και του Αγιου. (Καιφ. σ) /Prokeimenon of the saint of the day, Alleluia of the saint, Gospel of the day and of the saint (chapter 6)/. So also in chapter 99, Mark. Although this rubric is given only in the Saturday service, however, it must be understood that also on other days of the week it should be so. Slavonic typikons prescribe the whole service to the day and to the saint, if he has one, and troparia -- also to the temple, if it is of the Lord or of the Theotokos. The Moscow Typikon also prescribes to the temple, if the temple is of a saint (although this does not agree with the rubric of that same Typikon, given on September 26 and January 30); this same Typikon ends the Kontakia with the Kontakion for the Dead "With the saints give rest" with the refrain "Glory" and the Kontakion of the temple, if it is of the Lord or of the Theotokos, if -- not, then with the usual "Protection" ("O Undisputed Intercessor") with the refrain "Both now" (chapter 52). This custom we find also among Greek-Italians and Greek-Melkites, but it does not exist among Constantinopolitan Greeks and Athonites, with the exception of some. But, since according to the rubric of the Greek Typikon, two Apostles and two Gospels were to be taken, and according to the Slavonic rubric, also the whole service of the day and of the saint, if he has one, and, since many saints (over one hundred eighty) have an Apostle and Gospel, as follows from the content of the Apostle-Gospel, therefore, in order to shorten the duration of the divine service, followers of the Greek Rite began already many years ago to reject certain rubrics; therefore also our Ruthenians take at the Liturgy only the service of the day, and reject the saint without Polyeleos; some -- conversely: take only the service to the saint, and reject the service of the day, as evidenced by the Pochaiv Service Book of 1765, which gives the Apostle and Gospel only on Sundays, and on weekdays -- only in Bright Week and in the week after the Descent of the Holy Spirit, similarly as Latin Missals, which give the Apostle and Gospel of the day only in these two mentioned weeks, for Great Lent and for certain other days. This custom we find also among Greek-Italians, observed also by some of those who are called Orthodox, whom the Moscow Typikon accuses, saying: "The Ecumenical Church does not take neither on the Beatitudes, nor at Liturgies the Apostle and Gospel to the saint on every day, only the sequential, that is the pillar throughout the whole year, in sequence, as also of selected saints who have an Apostle and Gospel. And if the Apostle and Gospel of the saint is read every day, then this is from one's own ignorance, and not according to the church typikon" (chapter 58, at the end). Exactly so also in the manuscript of the three-hundred-year-old Gospel that is among us. However, this rubric of the Ecumenical Church is not observed by the Constantinopolitan Hierarch, called Ecumenical, nor by the Churches subject to him, who (according to the testimony of hierarch Benjamin Νεαπολεος /of Neapolis/, who lived in Constantinople and on Mount Athos, who in our times accepted church union) have the custom, also in monasteries, to take the Apostle and Gospel only of the day, if the saint does not have one, if he has, then only to the saint, and in another they adhere to the new Constantinopolitan Typikon, printed in the patriarchal typography in 1851, marked with the patriarchal seal and confirmed by signatures of all patriarchs and hierarchs subject to them. In this Typikon there, where the rubric gives two Apostles and Gospels, one Apostle-Gospel is given, that is the Apostle -- of the first service, and the Gospel -- of the second service. Thus e.g. October 26, November 21, when they fall on a Sunday and on John the Theologian May 8, when it falls on Ascension.
