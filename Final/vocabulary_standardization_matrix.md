## 4. Notes — Reconciled with System Instructions

### A. Book Title Policy (Established April 25, 2026)

All liturgical book titles are treated as proper names in their language of origin and are never translated. This gives a single uniform rule matching the existing Greek-title treatment (Octoechos, Triodion, Menaion, etc.):

| Original | Canonical | Literal Gloss (first occurrence only) |
|---|---|---|
| *Церковне Око* | **Tserkovne Oko** | (lit. "Eye of the Church") |
| *Служебник* | **Sluzhebnik** / **Sluzhebnyky** (pl.) | (lit. "Service Book(s)") |
| *Часословець і Молитвослов* | **Chasoslovets i Molitvoslov** | (lit. "Horo- and Prayer Book") |

**Rationale:** The system instructions mandated "Tserkovne Oko = Tserkovne Oko (Do not translate)." The earlier editorial override to "Eye of the Church" was reversed to maintain consistency with how all other book titles are handled (none are translated: Octoechos ≠ "Eight Tones"; Triodion ≠ "Three-Ode Book"; Horologion ≠ "Book of Hours"). The *Sluzhebnik* / *Sluzhebnyky* replacement was applied for the same reason, paralleling the already-untranslated *Euchologion* (the Greek equivalent of the same book type).
